Fei-Fei Li

The Woman Who Taught Computers to See

Discover the story of the computer scientist who pioneered a new era of artificial intelligence and computer vision.

Her Journey

Fei-Fei Li came to the United States from China as a teenager. Despite not speaking English well at first, she excelled academically and earned a scholarship to Princeton University.

She went on to earn her Ph.D. from Caltech, where she began her groundbreaking work in the field of artificial intelligence and computer vision.

The Birth of ImageNet

A Dataset for Machines

Li realized that for computers to "see" the world, they needed a massive amount of data to learn from. In 2009, she and her team at Princeton published **ImageNet**, a huge dataset of over 14 million labeled images.

The AI Catalyst

The creation of ImageNet and a yearly competition based on it sparked a new era of deep learning. By training on this dataset, AI models began to achieve incredible accuracy in recognizing objects, which was a pivotal moment for the field.

A Champion for Human-Centered AI

Today, Fei-Fei Li is a leading advocate for **human-centered AI**. She believes that AI should not just be powerful, but also beneficial and safe for humanity.

She co-founded **AI4ALL**, a non-profit organization dedicated to increasing diversity and inclusion in AI education, helping to build a more responsible and ethical future for the field.
