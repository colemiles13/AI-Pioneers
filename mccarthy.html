John McCarthy

The Father of AI

Explore the life of the computer scientist who gave artificial intelligence its name and helped shape its future.

The Birth of a Field

In the summer of 1956, John McCarthy and a small group of brilliant minds gathered at **Dartmouth College** for a workshop.

This meeting is considered the official birth of artificial intelligence as a field, and it was here that McCarthy first introduced the term **"Artificial Intelligence."**

Creating a New Language

A Language for AI

McCarthy understood that computers needed a different kind of language to handle the symbolic thinking required for AI. He created the **LISP** programming language.

A Lasting Legacy

LISP became the go-to language for AI research for many decades and influenced the design of many other programming languages.

A Vision for the Future

Time-Sharing

McCarthy was a pioneer in **time-sharing**, a technology that allowed multiple users to use a single computer at the same time by taking turns. This was a revolutionary idea.

Predicting the Cloud

He famously predicted that computing would eventually be a public utility, much like electricity or water. This is the basic idea behind **cloud computing**!
