Allen Newell

The Architect of AI

Explore the life and work of a founding father of artificial intelligence and cognitive science.

The Power of Collaboration

Newell's most impactful work was a result of his long-standing partnership with fellow computer scientist and economist **Herbert A. Simon**.

They were pioneers in creating computer programs that could simulate human problem-solving processes, laying the foundation for modern AI.

The Logic Theorist

The First AI Program

In 1956, Newell and Simon developed the **Logic Theorist**, widely considered the first true artificial intelligence program.

Thinking Like a Human

Instead of just computing, the program was designed to solve problems using heuristics, or "rules of thumb," much like a human would. It successfully proved 38 of the 52 theorems from the first chapter of a famous mathematics book.

The General Problem Solver (GPS)

A Universal Approach

Building on the Logic Theorist, they created GPS, a program designed to solve a wider range of problems by using a technique called **means-ends analysis**.

How it Worked

GPS worked by figuring out the difference between the current state and the goal state, and then finding a way to reduce that difference, a process that mimics how we solve everyday puzzles.

A Lasting Legacy

Newell and Simon's work led to the **physical symbol system hypothesis**, a foundational idea in AI that suggests that a system can show intelligence if it can manipulate symbols.
