Herbert Simon

The Polymath of AI

Discover the life of a Nobel Prize winner who helped invent artificial intelligence and changed our understanding of human decision-making.

A Legendary Partnership

Much of Simon's most famous work in AI was a collaboration with his colleague, **Allen Newell**.

Together, they created some of the very first AI programs, pushing the boundaries of what computers could do.

Pioneering AI

The Logic Theorist

In 1956, Simon and Newell created the **Logic Theorist**, a program that could prove mathematical theorems. It was one of the first programs to perform human-like reasoning.

The General Problem Solver (GPS)

They followed this up with GPS, a program designed to solve a wider variety of problems by mimicking how humans use logic and trial-and-error to find solutions.

A Nobel Prize in Economics

In 1978, Herbert Simon was awarded the Nobel Prize in Economics for his research into the decision-making process within economic organizations.

He developed the theory of **"bounded rationality,"** which argues that people's ability to make rational decisions is limited by the information they have and their cognitive skills.
